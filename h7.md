###Quintin (2014): 7 Things You Should Know About Tor

1) Tor protects anonymity: It hides a user’s IP address by routing internet traffic through multiple volunteer-operated servers around the world.

2) Core security remains strong: Despite concerns and surveillance disclosures, Tor’s fundamental cryptographic design has not been publicly broken. Most compromises occur through other weaknesses.

3) Not just for criminals: Tor is widely used by journalists, activists, whistleblowers, researchers, and ordinary users who want privacy or need to bypass censorship.

4) Open-source transparency: Although Tor was originally funded by the U.S. Navy, its code is open source and reviewed by independent researchers, reducing the likelihood of secret backdoors.

5) Easy to use: The Tor Browser allows users to access the Tor network without advanced technical knowledge by providing a preconfigured privacy-focused browser.

6) Slower browsing speeds: Traffic passes through multiple relays and encryption layers, which improves anonymity but reduces speed compared with normal internet browsing.

7) Anonymity depends on user behavior: Logging into personal accounts, downloading unsafe files, or misconfiguring software can reveal a user’s identity.

### Shavers & Bair (2016): Hiding Behind the Keyboard – The Tor Browser

Introduction: Tor enables anonymous communication online by concealing the user’s network identity. However, forensic evidence may still remain on the user’s device.

History and purpose: Tor was developed by the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory and later released as open-source software to help protect privacy and support secure communication.

How Tor works: It uses “onion routing,” where traffic is encrypted in layers and sent through a series of nodes (entry node, relay node, and exit node). Each node only knows limited information about the connection.

Tracking criminals using Tor: Law enforcement usually identifies suspects through operational mistakes, malware, undercover investigations, or forensic analysis rather than breaking Tor itself.
